Pulse

Pulse enables you can stay on top of your most important KPIs across all your dashboards and your latest builds. By adding important KPIs from your dashboards to Pulse, you can get a comprehensive picture of your data from a single location.

Pulse contains tiles that display information from your dashboards and the status of your builds.

Within Pulse, there are two types of tiles, data tiles that display your data's current status and recent history and build tiles that display the latest status of your builds.

You can access Pulse from the menu bar of the .

By hovering over the header of any tile in Pulse, you can view additional options for your tiles.

The icon opens a new tab in your browser. For data tiles the dashboard where the widget originated is displayed and for build tiles the Data Sources section in the Admin page is displayed.

The icon is a tooltip that describes the tile in more detail. For data tiles the tooltip describes who added the tile to Pulse and when it was added. Build tile tooltips describe when the tile was created, which is being monitored, and under what conditions an alert is triggered when alerts are enabled.

The icon displays a menu for managing tiles in Pulse. For more information regarding managing tiles in Pulse, see Managing Tiles in Pulse.

Adding Tiles to Pulse

To keep track of your important KPIs and builds, you add the relevant data and build tiles to Pulse.

The flow for adding tiles to Pulse is the same for creating alerts for Indicator widgets and builds. For more information about these flows, see the following topics:

For data tiles, see Creating Data Alerts.

For build tiles, see Creating Build Alerts.

Each of these flows adds tiles to Pulse and creates an alert. If you want to monitor your data and builds without alerts, see Deactivating Notifications.
